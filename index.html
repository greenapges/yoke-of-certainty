Yoke of Certainty

The weight of truth lies not in knowing, but in being certain of what you feel, which then becomes grounded beyond fellings.

I feel your humanity beyond measure because our similarities are extreme. Every circumstance you experience has, in some shape or form, become a part of your identity. That same circumstance drives us to feel the same. What differentiates us is our own desire to achieve a certain image and what we do attempting to achieve it, either suppressing or inciting those feelings.

Slowly each of us begins to believe a part of reality we desire. We see only what we think is beneficial to our perception of ourselves. A performative act towards oneself. Sudden realization has made me realize my incompatibility with the environment I once believed I belonged in.

Yet you precede your own principle through negligence. You are unknowingly drawn back to the very initial cause that led you to develop these principles.
